How It is Works:

    Step 1: Prepare Peel Extract

  1. Collect fresh fruit/vegetable peels.
  2. Blend or crush them with a small amount of water.
  1. Filter to get a liquid extract. This juice contains natural acids, salts, and antioxidants – perfect for ionic conduction.

    Step 2: Add Hydrogen Peroxide

  1. Add a small amount of 3–5% hydrogen peroxide to the extract (ratio: ~1 part H₂O₂ to 4 parts peel extract). H₂O₂ helps boost the oxidation reactions.

    Step 3: Set Up Electrodes

  1. Insert a zinc strip (anode) and a copper strip (cathode) into the solution.
  2. Make sure the strips don't touch each other.
  3. Connect each metal to wires using alligator clips.

    Step 4: Complete the Circuit

  1. Connect the wires to a multimeter to measure voltage or to a small LED (optional). You should see a voltage (~0.4–0.8V per cell depending on peel type and peroxide amount).

    Step 5: (Optional) Increase Power

  1. To increase voltage or current, connect multiple such cells in series or parallel.

Working Principle:

Zinc (Anode) loses electrons (oxidation):
Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻

Hydrogen peroxide + Cu (Cathode) gains electrons (reduction):
H₂O₂ + 2H⁺ + 2e⁻ → 2H₂O

The peel extract acts as the electrolyte, enabling ion flow.
